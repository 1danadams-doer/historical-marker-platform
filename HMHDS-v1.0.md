# Historical Marker & Heritage Data Standard (HMHDS)

## Version 1.0

### Draft

### June 2026

---

## Mission Statement

The Historical Marker & Heritage Data Standard exists to preserve, connect, and share the stories of people, places, events, organizations, and historical resources for future generations.

Historical markers often serve as the first public entry point into local history. Behind every marker lies a larger story: communities, families, buildings, landscapes, institutions, photographs, documents, oral histories, and educational resources that are often fragmented across organizations or at risk of being lost.

The purpose of this standard is not merely to catalog markers. Its purpose is to create a durable framework that allows historical societies, museums, libraries, educators, researchers, students, volunteers, and the public to preserve and share historical knowledge regardless of technical expertise or financial resources.

The long-term vision is a global research and education platform where historical markers become gateways to a broader network of connected historical information.

The platform should enable even the smallest local historical society to preserve and share its knowledge without requiring specialized technical staff, database expertise, GIS knowledge, or dedicated funding.

All technology decisions, data models, and governance policies should support this mission.

The platform seeks not only to preserve historical information, but also to support the ongoing stewardship, restoration, and public awareness of historical resources that might otherwise be overlooked, neglected, or lost.

Historical knowledge provides its greatest value when it is accessible and reusable. The platform therefore seeks to make historical information available for educational, research, public-interest, nonprofit, and commercial applications whenever licensing and source restrictions permit.

---

## Public Benefit

The platform exists to:

1. Preserve historical knowledge.
2. Support historical education.
3. Enable historical research.
4. Promote stewardship and restoration.
5. Encourage community participation.
6. Enable responsible reuse of historical information.
7. Return value to the preservation community.

---

## Vision

We envision a future in which every historical marker serves as a doorway into a living historical knowledge network.

Historical knowledge should be available wherever people are learning, traveling, researching, teaching, or exploring—not solely within the platform itself.

---

## Purpose

The Historical Marker & Heritage Data Standard establishes a common framework for documenting, preserving, researching, and sharing information about historical markers, plaques, tablets, monuments, memorials, historic buildings, historic sites, landmarks, historic districts, interpretive signs, archaeological resources, trails, heritage routes, and related cultural and historical resources.

---

## Core Concept

Historical markers are not the destination. They are entry points.

The primary purpose of the system is to preserve and connect historical knowledge.

The standard centers on Historical Resources rather than markers alone.

---

## Design Principles

### Honesty Over Completeness

The platform must never imply certainty where none exists.

### Provenance First

Every significant fact must be traceable to a source.

### Source Preservation

Original source records must be preserved exactly as received whenever practical.

### Automation First, Verification Second

The platform should automate metadata generation, extraction, classification, relationship discovery, and data entry whenever practical.

Contributors should be asked to review, confirm, correct, or enrich information rather than manually creating structured records.

### Open and Responsible Reuse

Historical information should be available for reuse whenever licensing and source restrictions permit. Attribution, provenance, and rights information must remain visible.

---

## Identifier Standard

Format:

`HM-CC-SS-NNNNNNNN`

Examples:

- `HM-US-TX-00000001`
- `HM-US-NH-00000001`
- `HM-CA-ON-00000001`

Identifiers must be globally unique, never reused, never reassigned, stable across relocations, and must not encode county or municipal geography.

---

## Community Preservation

The platform is intended to complement—not replace—official programs by creating a distributed network of contributors who can help document historical resources, identify missing information, report changes in condition, preserve local knowledge, and improve public awareness.

---

## Open Access and Reuse

The platform is intended to support public, educational, nonprofit, research, and commercial use of historical information whenever licensing and source restrictions permit.

Where source licensing permits, the platform should make data accessible through open standards, documented APIs, exports, and integration mechanisms.

---

## Guiding Principle

The platform exists to ensure that local history can be preserved, connected, discovered, shared, reused, and protected regardless of the size, budget, or technical capability of the organization that holds the knowledge.
